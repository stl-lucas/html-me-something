What is Brewing Beer?

Brewing beer is the process of milling and steeping grain, boiling the liquid with hops, chilling, adding yeast, and waiting for fermentation to complete. It can be a fun way to make great tasting beer to share with friends and family.

Many new homebrewers are suprised to find how similar brewing a small 5 gallon batch of beer at home is to the brewing process behind some of their commercial favorites. There are many styles of beer to choose from and a few different ways to brew the beer. Most beginners start out with the process of extract brewing and more advanced brewers choose the "all-grain" method. All styles of beer can be categorized as a Ale or Lager. Lager styles take a little more expertise, time and equipment so most homebrewers stick to brewing Ales. Let's go over some thing you'll need to make 5 gallons of beer.

What Do You Need?

In order to brew a batch of beer you will need a few key pieces of equipment & ingredients.

  • Brew Kettle (10g Stainless Steel)
  • Fermentation Vessel (6.5g plastic bucket with lid)
  • Fermentation Lock
  • Wort Chiller
  • Paddle or Stirring Spoon
  • Strainer
  • Ingredient Kit (Malt, Hops, Yeast & Water)
  • Timer & Notepad
  • 48 - 52 Empty Bottles
  • Bottle Caps and Capper

How Do You Brew?

  1. Heat 6 or 7 gallons of water to a boil. Before reaching a boil, add your malt extract and stir in thoroughly.
  2. When you reach a boil, add hops and start a timer for 60 minutes.
  3. After 60 minutes, chill the wort (liquid) to below 80 degrees.
  4. Pour into your fermentation bucket and add yeast.
  5. Seal your fermentator and place in 60 - 65 degree for about 2 weeks.
  6. Bottle the beer with priming sure and let sit for at least 1 week before drinking.
  7. Enjoy!
